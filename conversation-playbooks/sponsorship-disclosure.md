# Sponsorship Disclosure Playbook

**Positioning:** Enterprise Technology Analyst — AI Automation, Cloud Security & Data Analytics  
**Tone:** Transparent, confident, matter-of-fact — not apologetic, not defensive

---

## Core Principle

Disclose work authorization **when asked or when it becomes relevant** — not in the first sentence of every message. Lead with skills and portfolio; be transparent when the conversation reaches authorization.

---

## When to Disclose

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Application form asks about authorization | Answer honestly: "Authorized to work with OPT/EAD; may require future sponsorship" |
| Recruiter asks directly | Use script below |
| Interview scheduling (HR screen) | Proactive disclosure — saves everyone's time |
| Networking (informational) | Only if they ask or if discussing specific role requirements |
| Job description says "no sponsorship" | Skip unless you have strong reason to believe otherwise; log as cold company |

---

## Disclosure Script (Recruiter / HR)

> I'm currently authorized to work in the U.S. on Optional Practical Training (OPT) with an Employment Authorization Document (EAD), valid through [month/year]. For long-term employment, I would require visa sponsorship. I've researched [Company]'s public sponsorship history and it appears promising, but I understand each role is evaluated individually. I'm committed to contributing value from day one and am happy to discuss timeline details if we're aligned on role fit.

---

## Disclosure Script (Hiring Manager)

> I want to be transparent about work authorization since it's relevant to long-term planning. I'm on OPT with EAD through [date]. I'm not looking for a short-term role — I'm building a career in enterprise technology and [Company] is a deliberate target based on my research. If sponsorship is feasible for this team, I'd love to continue our conversation. If not, I'd rather know now so we both save time.

---

## What NOT to Say

- ❌ "I desperately need sponsorship"
- ❌ "I'll work for anything if you sponsor me"
- ❌ "Sponsorship is my only concern"
- ❌ Hiding authorization status until offer stage
- ❌ Arguing with "no sponsorship" job postings

---

## Research Before Disclosure

1. Check CI OS Sponsorship Signal tab for employer-level signal
2. Verify via public DOL/USCIS H-1B disclosure data (not included in MVP — manual step)
3. Review company packet sponsorship notes
4. Check conversation log for prior insights about this company's sponsorship approach

---

## After Disclosure — Read the Response

| Response | Next Action |
|----------|-------------|
| "No problem, we sponsor regularly" | Continue with confidence; log as warm |
| "It depends on the role/team" | Ask which teams typically sponsor; log insight |
| "We can't sponsor for this role" | Thank them professionally; mark company as cold for this role |
| No response after disclosure | Follow up once after 5 days; then move on |

Log every sponsorship conversation in `data/conversation_log_template.csv` with `insight_gained` capturing their response.
