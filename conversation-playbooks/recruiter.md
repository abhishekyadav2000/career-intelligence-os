# Recruiter Conversation Playbook

**Positioning:** Enterprise Technology Analyst — AI Automation, Cloud Security & Data Analytics  
**Tone:** Professional, collaborative, confident — not needy, not spammy

---

## Goals

1. Establish credibility with a specific, relevant portfolio artifact
2. Reference a concrete role (not generic "any opening")
3. Make it easy for the recruiter to forward your profile
4. Be transparent about work authorization when asked — not preemptively defensive

---

## Opening (LinkedIn / Email)

> Hi [Name], I noticed the [Specific Role Title] opening at [Company] in [Location]. I built a career intelligence system that demonstrates the Python, SQL, and cloud security skills this role requires — with a working Streamlit dashboard and explainable scoring. I'd welcome a brief conversation about how my background maps to your team's current needs.

**Why this works:** Specific role + concrete proof + low-friction ask (brief conversation, not "please hire me").

---

## If They Ask About Work Authorization

> I'm currently authorized to work in the U.S. on OPT with EAD, valid through [date]. I'm transparent about sponsorship needs and happy to discuss timeline if the role is a strong mutual fit. I've found that early transparency saves time for both sides.

See [sponsorship-disclosure.md](sponsorship-disclosure.md) for full guidance.

---

## If They Ask "Tell Me About Yourself"

> I'm an enterprise technology analyst focused on the intersection of AI automation, cloud security, and data analytics. I built Career Intelligence OS — a decision system that scores role fit across 50 DFW employers, generates outreach strategy, and maps interview prep topics. It demonstrates the Python, SQL, and risk-aware thinking I bring to technology analyst roles. I'm particularly interested in [Company]'s work on [specific initiative from company packet].

---

## If They Ask About Salary

> I'm flexible and focused on finding the right role fit first. I'd appreciate understanding the range for this position so we can determine alignment early.

---

## If They Ask "Why This Company?"

Use talking points from the company packet. Example:

> [Company]'s DFW technology center and focus on [platform modernization / cloud security / data analytics] align with my portfolio work. I prioritized [Company] in my targeting system based on role fit, sponsorship signal, and contact network — it's a deliberate choice, not a mass application.

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

- ❌ "I'm desperately looking for sponsorship"
- ❌ Generic copy-paste without role/company specificity
- ❌ Attaching résumé without context in first message
- ❌ Multiple follow-ups within 48 hours
- ❌ Overselling — let the portfolio demo speak

---

## Follow-Up Timing

| Situation | Wait | Action |
|-----------|------|--------|
| No reply | 5–7 business days | One polite follow-up with new value (demo link, relevant article) |
| Replied, scheduling | 24 hours | Confirm time + send portfolio link |
| Replied, not a fit | Same day | Thank them; ask if they know another team/role |
| Interview scheduled | 24 hours before | Confirm + review company packet + interview packet |
