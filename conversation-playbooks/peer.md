# Peer Conversation Playbook

**Positioning:** Enterprise Technology Analyst — AI Automation, Cloud Security & Data Analytics  
**Tone:** Curious, respectful, informational — not asking for referrals upfront

---

## Goals

1. Understand day-to-day reality of the role (not just job description)
2. Learn team culture, tools, and growth paths
3. Build genuine connection — referrals come from trust, not asks
4. Gather insider vocabulary for interview prep

---

## Opening (LinkedIn / Meetup / Alumni Network)

> Hi [Name], I noticed you're a [Title] at [Company] — I'm exploring similar paths in enterprise technology (AI automation, cloud security, data analytics). Would you be open to a 15-minute informational chat about your experience? No pressure on referrals — I'm focused on understanding the role landscape.

---

## Questions to Ask

### Day-to-Day
1. "What does a typical week look like for you?"
2. "What tools and platforms does your team use most?"
3. "How much of your time is hands-on technical vs. meetings and stakeholder communication?"

### Growth & Culture
4. "What skills have been most valuable for your career progression?"
5. "How does your team handle learning and onboarding for new analysts?"
6. "What's one thing you wish you'd known before joining?"

### Industry Insight
7. "How is [Company] approaching AI automation — cautiously or aggressively?"
8. "What's the biggest challenge your team faced in the last year?"
9. "Are there teams or roles you'd recommend I explore based on my background?"

---

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Ask for a referral in the first message
- ❌ Complain about job search difficulty
- ❌ Overshare about sponsorship stress
- ❌ Treat it as a disguised interview
- ❌ Go over 20 minutes without checking their time

---

## After the Conversation

1. Send a thank-you message within 24 hours
2. Log insights in `data/conversation_log_template.csv`:
   - `insight_gained`: vocabulary, team structure, tool names
   - `portfolio_gap`: anything they mentioned you should demonstrate
3. If the conversation was warm, wait 2–3 weeks before asking about referrals — and only if a specific role opens

---

## Sample Thank-You

> Hi [Name], thank you for taking the time to share your experience at [Company]. Your insight about [specific thing they said] was really helpful — I'm going to [specific action based on their advice]. I appreciate your openness and hope we can stay connected.
