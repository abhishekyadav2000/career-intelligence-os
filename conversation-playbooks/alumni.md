# Alumni Conversation Playbook

**Positioning:** Enterprise Technology Analyst — AI Automation, Cloud Security & Data Analytics  
**Tone:** Warm, shared-connection, professional — leverage common ground without being presumptuous

---

## Goals

1. Activate the alumni network as a warm channel (higher response rate than cold outreach)
2. Get insider perspective on company culture and hiring process
3. Earn a warm intro to hiring manager or recruiter (indirectly, through trust)
4. Demonstrate you've done homework on the company

---

## Opening

> Hi [Name], fellow [University] alum here — I saw you're at [Company] as a [Title]. I'm exploring enterprise technology analyst roles in DFW (AI automation, cloud security, data analytics) and [Company] is a top target in my research. Would you be open to a brief chat about your experience there? Happy to work around your schedule.

**Why alumni works:** Shared identity lowers barrier; "top target in my research" shows intentionality.

---

## Conversation Flow

### 1. Connect (2 min)
- Reference shared university experience (major, club, professor — if genuine)
- Mention why you chose to reach out to them specifically (their role, their path)

### 2. Learn (10 min)
- "What drew you to [Company]?"
- "How does the DFW office compare to other locations?"
- "What's the hiring process like for [role family]?"
- "Any teams you'd recommend I explore?"

### 3. Share (3 min)
- Brief portfolio mention: "I built a career intelligence system that actually ranked [Company] in my top 5 based on role fit and sponsorship signal."
- Offer to share demo link if they're curious

### 4. Close (2 min)
- Thank them genuinely
- Ask: "Would it be appropriate to mention our conversation if I apply to a specific role?"
- Do NOT ask for a direct referral unless they offer

---

## Alumni-Specific Advantages

| Advantage | How to Use It |
|-----------|--------------|
| Higher response rate | Lead with alumni identity in subject line |
| Insider hiring process knowledge | Ask about timeline, interview format, team structure |
| Cultural fit signal | Ask about work-life balance, team dynamics honestly |
| Warm intro potential | Earn it through genuine conversation, not direct ask |

---

## If They Offer to Refer

> That would be incredibly helpful — thank you. I'll apply to [specific role] and send you my portfolio link so you have context to share. I want to make sure I'm representing myself well since your name is attached.

---

## If They Can't Help

> Completely understand — I appreciate you even taking the time to respond. If you know anyone in [adjacent team/company] who might be open to a brief chat, I'd welcome an intro, but no pressure at all.
