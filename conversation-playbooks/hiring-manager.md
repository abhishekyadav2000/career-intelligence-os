# Hiring Manager Conversation Playbook

**Positioning:** Enterprise Technology Analyst — AI Automation, Cloud Security & Data Analytics  
**Tone:** Technical depth, business awareness, collaborative — peer-level, not subordinate

---

## Goals

1. Demonstrate you understand their team's problems (not just job requirements)
2. Show working artifacts, not just résumé bullets
3. Ask intelligent questions about priorities and team structure
4. Leave them with a reason to advocate for you internally

---

## Opening (Informational / Referral Intro)

> Hi [Name], [Referrer name] suggested I reach out. I'm exploring [Role Family] opportunities at [Company] and built a portfolio project — Career Intelligence OS — that scores enterprise role fit using Python, SQL, and explainable analytics. I'd appreciate 15 minutes to learn about your team's current priorities around [platform modernization / cloud security / data analytics] and share how my work maps.

---

## Technical Depth Questions to Ask

1. "What's the biggest data pipeline or automation challenge your team is working on right now?"
2. "How does your team balance speed of delivery with security controls and audit readiness?"
3. "What does a typical week look like for an analyst on your team — split between hands-on technical work and stakeholder communication?"
4. "Are you evaluating any AI automation tools, and if so, what guardrails matter most?"

---

## How to Present Your Portfolio

**Structure (5 minutes max):**

1. **Problem:** "Enterprise job search lacks structured decision-making — I built a system to fix that."
2. **Demo:** Show Overview tab → Company Ranking → Role Fit drill-down (2 min)
3. **Depth:** Mention companion case studies (AI agent risk scoring, cloud security evidence lab)
4. **Connection:** "This is the same structured thinking I'd bring to [specific team problem they mentioned]."

**Do not:** Walk through every tab. Pick 3 that match their team's domain.

---

## If They Challenge Your Experience Level

> I understand I'm early in my enterprise career. What I've focused on is demonstrating the underlying skills — Python data pipelines, SQL analytics, explainable scoring, and risk-aware design — through working artifacts rather than years of tenure. I'm a fast learner and my portfolio shows I can ship structured solutions, not just talk about them.

---

## If They Ask About Sponsorship

> I'm on OPT with EAD, valid through [date]. I want to be upfront — if the role requires sponsorship, I'd like to understand the company's typical timeline and process. I've researched [Company]'s sponsorship history via public DOL data and it looks promising, but I know each role is different.

---

## Closing

> Thank you for your time. Based on what you shared about [specific priority], I'll [specific follow-up action — e.g., prepare an IAM walkthrough, review a specific module]. Would it be appropriate to stay in touch as relevant openings come up?

Log the conversation immediately in `data/conversation_log_template.csv` — capture insights, portfolio gaps, and next actions.
