# Problem Statement

## Context

The Dallas-Fort Worth metro area hosts 50+ enterprise technology employers with active hiring in cloud security, data analytics, and AI automation. For international talent on OPT/EAD, the decision landscape is especially complex: role fit, sponsorship history, networking paths, and posting quality all interact — but no unified system helps candidates or analysts make structured decisions.

## Problem

1. **Fragmented intelligence** — Company, job, and contact data live in spreadsheets with no scoring or prioritization.
2. **Opaque sponsorship signals** — H-1B/PERM history is hard to interpret; candidates either over-trust or ignore signals.
3. **Noise in job postings** — Templated, stale, or "ghost" listings waste application effort.
4. **No action framework** — Without recommendations (apply, network, research, skip), effort is distributed evenly instead of strategically.
5. **Missing audit trail** — Decisions aren't traceable; rationale isn't documented.

## Impact

- Low response rates on cold applications
- Missed warm-intro opportunities at Tier-1 employers
- Time spent on low-fit or high-noise roles
- Inability to demonstrate analytical decision-making to employers

## Success Criteria (MVP)

- Load and validate 50 companies, 100+ jobs, 75+ contacts
- Score roles across six decision categories
- Generate actionable recommendations with rationale
- Surface sponsorship signals with legal disclaimers
- Provide exportable results and SQL analytics demo
